## What is an SEO Audit?

An SEO audit is a comprehensive analysis of your website's technical health, content quality, and search engine visibility. It helps identify issues that might be preventing your site from ranking higher in search results.

### Key Areas to Audit

**Technical SEO**
- Page load speed and performance
- Mobile responsiveness
- HTTPS security
- Structured data and schema markup

**On-Page SEO**
- Meta tags (title, description)
- Heading hierarchy (H1-H6)
- Image alt text
- Internal linking structure

**Accessibility**
- WCAG compliance
- Color contrast ratios
- Screen reader compatibility
- Keyboard navigation

## Common SEO Issues to Watch For

Here are some frequent problems MetaBear helps you catch:

1. **Missing or duplicate meta descriptions** - Every page needs a unique, compelling description
2. **Poor heading hierarchy** - Skipping heading levels confuses search engines and users
3. **Missing alt text** - Images without descriptions hurt both SEO and accessibility
4. **Broken links** - Dead links damage user experience and SEO
5. **Low contrast text** - Fails accessibility standards and hurts readability

## Best Practices for Regular Audits

**After Major Changes**
Run an audit whenever you update your site's design, content structure, or technical infrastructure.

**Monthly Check-ins**
Schedule monthly audits for high-priority pages like your homepage, product pages, and top-performing content.

**Before Publishing**
Audit new content before it goes live to catch issues early.

**Competitor Analysis**
Use MetaBear to analyze competitor sites and identify opportunities for improvement.
